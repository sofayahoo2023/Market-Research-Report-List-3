<p><p>Long fiber reinforced polypropylene (LFT) typically consists of continuous fibers that are aligned along the length of the plastic, providing excellent strength and stiffness. Short fiber reinforced polypropylene (SFT) on the other hand, consists of shorter fibers randomly dispersed within the plastic, offering improved impact resistance and dimensional stability. Both types have their own unique characteristics and are used in various automotive applications based on specific requirements for strength, stiffness, impact resistance, and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
