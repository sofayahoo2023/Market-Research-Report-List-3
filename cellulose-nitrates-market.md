<p><p>Cellulose nitrates are commonly classified into three main market types based on their nitration level: E-grade nitrocellulose, M-grade nitrocellulose, and A-grade nitrocellulose. E-grade nitrocellulose has the lowest nitrogen content and is typically used in the production of paints and coatings. M-grade nitrocellulose has a medium nitrogen content and is often used in the manufacturing of explosives. A-grade nitrocellulose has the highest nitrogen content and is mainly utilized in the production of celluloid and lacquers. Other markets for cellulose nitrates include pharmaceuticals, adhesives, and plastics.</p></p>
