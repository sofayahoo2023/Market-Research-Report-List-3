<p><p>Insulation Gate Field Effect Transistors (IGFETs) are divided into two main types based on the type of majority charge carrier they use: N-Type IGFETs and P-Type IGFETs. </p><p>N-Type IGFETs have a negative charge carrier (electrons) as the majority charge carriers, while P-Type IGFETs have a positive charge carrier (holes) as the majority charge carriers. Both types of IGFETs are used in electronic devices to control the flow of current, with N-Type IGFETs typically used in high-speed and high-power applications, while P-Type IGFETs are used in low-power applications.</p></p>
