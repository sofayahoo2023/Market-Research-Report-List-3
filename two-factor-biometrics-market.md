<p><p>Two-factor biometrics refers to a security measure that combines two different authentication methods to ensure a higher level of security. In the context of passwords, it involves the use of a username/password combination along with an additional factor, such as a one-time password (OTP). Hard tokens are physical devices, like key fobs or smart cards, that generate unique codes for authentication. Soft tokens, on the other hand, are digital versions of hard tokens that can be stored on a mobile device. Biometrics market encompasses the use of unique physical or behavioral characteristics, like fingerprints or facial recognition, for authentication purposes.</p></p>
