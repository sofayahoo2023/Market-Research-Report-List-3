<p><p>Car rear view cameras are widely available in two main types: wired and wireless. Wired cameras are connected to the vehicle's electrical system for power and data transmission. They are typically more reliable and provide a constant feed of video footage. Wireless cameras, on the other hand, use a wireless signal to transmit video footage to a display inside the vehicle. They are easier to install but may experience interference or signal loss. Both types offer drivers added visibility and safety while reversing or parking.</p></p>
