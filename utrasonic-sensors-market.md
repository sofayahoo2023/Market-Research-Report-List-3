<p><p>Ultrasonic sensors are commonly found in three main market types: proximity sensors, retro-reflective sensors, and through-beam sensors. Proximity sensors detect the presence of an object within a certain range, while retro-reflective sensors use a reflector to bounce the ultrasonic waves back to the sensor. Through-beam sensors consist of a transmitter and receiver placed opposite each other to detect objects passing between them. Each type of sensor has its own unique applications and advantages in various industries.</p></p>
