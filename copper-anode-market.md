<p><p>Copper anodes are divided into two main types - Phosphor Copper Anode and Oxygen Free Copper Anode. Phosphor Copper Anode contains a small amount of phosphorus, which enhances its strength and electrical conductivity. On the other hand, Oxygen Free Copper Anode has a minimal amount of oxygen, resulting in high conductivity and resistance to corrosion. Both types are widely used in industries such as electronics, construction, and manufacturing for various applications requiring high-quality copper material.</p></p>
