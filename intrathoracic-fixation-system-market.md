<p><p>The Intrathoracic Fixation System is a medical device designed to provide stability and fixation of the sternum and ribs following sternotomy or rib fractures. It is commonly used in cardiothoracic surgeries, including coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), valve repair or replacement, and lung transplantation.</p><p>The market for Intrathoracic Fixation System is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.3% during the forecast period. Manufacturers are focusing on developing innovative systems that offer improved stability, ease of use, and reduced risk of complications. For instance, some companies are introducing minimally invasive fixation systems that require smaller incisions and provide better patient outcomes.</p><p>Another trend in the market is the increasing adoption of bioresorbable fixation systems. These systems are made of biocompatible materials that gradually degrade over time, eliminating the need for removal surgeries. The bioresorbable systems offer advantages such as reduced risk of infections, improved patient comfort, and better postoperative recovery.</p><p>Geographically, North America is expected to dominate the Intrathoracic Fixation System market, owing to the high prevalence of cardiac disorders, well-established healthcare infrastructure, and favorable reimbursement policies.
