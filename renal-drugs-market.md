<p><p>The renal drugs market consists of two main types: oral medications and injections. Oral medications are taken by mouth and are typically in the form of tablets or capsules. They are convenient and easy to administer and can be taken at home without the need for medical assistance. They generally have a faster onset of action and are commonly used when immediate effects are required or when oral medications are not suitable or tolerated by the patient. </p><p>These two types of drugs cater to different patient needs and preferences, and healthcare providers decide on the most appropriate administration route based on various factors such as the severity of the condition, patient compliance, and the specific drug being prescribed.</p></p>
