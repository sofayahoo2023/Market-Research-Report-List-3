<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Viral Vectors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Retroviral Vectors</li><li>Lentiviral Vectors</li><li>Adenoviral Vectors</li><li>Herpes Simplex Virus Vectors</li><li>Chimeric Viral Vectors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Viral vectors are widely used in gene therapy and vaccine development. Retroviral vectors, derived from RNA viruses, integrate their genetic material into the host cell genome. Lentiviral vectors, a subset of retroviral vectors, allow efficient transduction of non-dividing cells. Adenoviral vectors, derived from DNA viruses, offer high gene transfer efficiency but are only transiently expressed. Herpes Simplex Virus vectors are effective at delivering genes to neurons. Chimeric viral vectors combine elements from multiple viruses to enhance efficiency and specificity. Other viral vectors include those derived from adeno-associated viruses, vaccinia viruses, and vesicular stomatitis viruses.</p></p>
