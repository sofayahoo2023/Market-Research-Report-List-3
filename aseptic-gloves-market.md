<p><p>Aseptic gloves are available in four main types - emulsion, nitrile, neoprene, and polyisoprene. Emulsion gloves are made from natural rubber latex and are commonly used in healthcare settings. Nitrile gloves are a synthetic alternative to latex and are suitable for those with latex allergies. Neoprene gloves offer excellent chemical resistance and durability. Polyisoprene gloves are similar to latex but are free from natural rubber proteins, making them a good option for those with latex sensitivities. Each type of glove serves specific needs in the aseptic market.</p></p>
