<p><p>The Surface Acoustic Wave (SAW) sensor market includes two main types: resonators and delay lines. Resonators are used to measure frequency changes caused by external stimuli, making them ideal for sensing small changes in physical parameters. Delay lines, on the other hand, measure the time it takes for a signal to travel between two points, allowing for precise measurement of time-sensitive events. Both types of SAW sensors are widely used in various industries for applications like temperature sensing, pressure monitoring, and gas detection.</p></p>
