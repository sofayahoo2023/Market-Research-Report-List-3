<p><p>Dissolved oxygen sensors for semiconductor market types include the polarography method, current method, and optical method. The polarography method measures oxygen concentration through electrochemical reactions, while the current method uses a probe to measure the oxygen-related current. The optical method utilizes light absorption or fluorescence to determine oxygen levels. Each method offers unique advantages and applications within the semiconductor industry, providing accurate and reliable monitoring of dissolved oxygen levels in various processes.</p></p>
