<p><p>The flatbed laser cutting service market offers various types of cutting technologies. CO2 laser cutting is a popular method that uses a carbon dioxide laser to cut materials like wood, acrylic, paper, and fabric. Fiber optic laser cutting relies on a high-powered fiber laser to cut metals with precision and speed. Waterjet cutting utilizes a high-pressure water stream mixed with abrasive particles to cut through materials like metal, stone, and composites. Plasma cutting involves the use of a high-temperature plasma torch to cut through electrically conductive materials such as steel, aluminum, and brass.</p></p>
