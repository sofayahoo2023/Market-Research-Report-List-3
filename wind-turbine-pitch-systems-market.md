<p><p>Wind turbines are typically equipped with either hydraulic pitch systems or electrical pitch systems to control the angle of the turbine blades in response to changing wind conditions. Hydraulic pitch systems utilize hydraulic actuators to adjust the blade angle, while electrical pitch systems rely on electric motors. Both systems play a crucial role in maximizing the efficiency of the wind turbine by ensuring that the blades are optimally positioned to capture the most energy from the wind.</p></p>
