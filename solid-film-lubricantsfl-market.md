<p><p>Solid Film Lubricants (SFL) are used to reduce friction and wear between surfaces in various industries. There are three main types in the market: PTFE Type, Molybdenum Disulfide Type, and Graphite Type. PTFE Type SFLs are made of polytetrafluoroethylene that provides low friction and excellent chemical resistance. Molybdenum Disulfide Type SFLs contain solid lubricant particles that adhere well to surfaces, reducing friction. Graphite Type SFLs are composed of graphite particles that provide good lubrication properties. Each type serves specific applications based on their unique characteristics.</p></p>
